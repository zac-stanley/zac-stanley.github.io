Wildfires in California

This bivariate map represents wildfires in California from 2009-2019. The symbol size represents size in acres >= 100 and the colors represent the five most common cause of fire during this time period. The data came from the U.S. Forest Service; FIRESTAT Fire Occurrence - Yearly Update.

Pacific Crest Trail Peaks

This map shows the Pacific Crest Trail from Mexico to Canada, and highlights the major mountain peaks along the route. It is overlaid on a geoferenced version of the classic Erwin Raisz Landforms of the United States Map.

Home <--> Work by Bike

This map highlights a bike route from my home in Petaluma, CA to my office, 22 miles south in San Rafael, CA. The critical points along the way are my kid's school and a bike shop/watering hole that I sometimes in on my way home on Fridays.

United States Power Plants

This bivariate map shows power plants in the United States using graduated symbols to show total power capacity in megawatts and color to describe the primary source of power; Nuclear, Coal or Petroleum.

Eat-Play-Walk Minneapolis!

Map of Cafe's or Restaraunts within 1-mile of an outdoor recreation site in Minneapolis, MN. The area in the mauve color has more than one cafe' or restaraunt within 1-mile of a recreation site and the area in the golden color has a at least one cafe' or restaraunt within 1-mile of a recreation site. I used purple for amenities as a nod to hometown musical genius, Prince. All data from Open Street Map, 2019.

About me

I'm Zac Stanley, a geographer and cartographer specializing in high quality static cartography and web mapping applications. My area of interest includes mapping trails, invasive species, and a broad array of natural resource monitoring efforts from wildlife cameras to rare plants and native gardens.
When I'm not looking for the perfect complimentary font for a map sub-title, I'm hanging out with my family, riding my bike in Western Sonoma County California, or listening to the hypnotic tenor saxophone of Pharoah Sanders.
